TiXL is an open source software for creating realtime motion graphics. It targets the sweet spot between real-time rendering, graph-based procedural content generation, and linear keyframe animation — giving artists the flexibility to build anything from reactive VJ visuals to precisely timed motion graphics sequences.

## What TiXL is

At its core, TiXL is a **node-based compositor and animation environment** running on DirectX 11. You build visuals by connecting operators in a graph canvas. Each operator is a self-contained computation: a mesh generator, a shader effect, a texture transform, a math node. Connecting operators creates a live evaluation graph that renders in real time.

Keyframe animation runs alongside the graph. You can animate any operator parameter on a timeline, combine keyframes with procedural automation, or drive parameters from audio analysis and external signals — all at the same time.

## Who it's for

- **VJs and live performers** who need audio-reactive visuals that respond to music in real time
- **Motion designers** who want procedural, non-destructive workflows with timeline-based control
- **Technical artists** who write HLSL shaders, build custom operators, or integrate hardware controllers
- **Creative tinkerers** exploring generative art, data visualization, or interactive installations

## Key capabilities

**Rendering and shaders**
DirectX 11 rendering pipeline with support for fragment shaders, compute shaders, and full HLSL shader authoring directly inside the editor. Color correction, tone mapping, and scopes are built in.

**Audio reactivity**
Audio analysis operators expose frequency bands, beat detection, and amplitude envelopes as graph values you can wire into any parameter.

**External I/O**
Connect MIDI controllers, send and receive OSC messages, and integrate DMX for lighting control. Spout and NDI output let you route frames to other applications and network endpoints.

**Standalone export**
Package a finished project as a small self-contained executable (the Player). The exported binary embeds your operator graph and assets, and accepts command-line flags for resolution, windowed mode, vsync, and looping.

**HLSL shader development**
Write and hot-reload HLSL shaders without leaving TiXL. The built-in shader compiler gives live feedback on errors.
